Lady Of The Arabic Singing , Star Of The East , Soma

considered one of the greatest Arab musicians who ever lived. For over fifty years, she stunned the Arab world with her unmatchable voice; her vocals able to penetrate people’s hearts and express the soul of nations. Her lyrics were written by poets who eagerly offered their pieces to her in hopes of becoming the next esteemed songwriter ordained into Umm Kulthum’s repertoire

Death

Umm Kulthum dies from heart failure at around age 70. Millions of Egyptians and people from around the world crowd the streets of Cairo to pay their final respects at her funeral

Umm Kulthum is knighted - 1944s

King Farouk grants Umm Kulthum with the highest Egyptian order of Knighthood. The Egyptian order of Knighthood (Nishan Al-Kamal) had previously been reserved for royalty and politicians.

Umm Kulthum tours for Egypt 1967 - 1971

After the Six-Day war defeat, she travelled throughout Egypt and the Arab world, donating the proceeds of her performances to the government of Egypt. These concerts were much publicized and took on the character of state visits

Umm Kulthum partners with Abdel Wahab

Umm Kulthum partners with Mohammed Abdel Wahab 1965 As a contemporary of Umm Kulthum, Abdel Wahab was a highly regarded singer and songwriter who composed thousands of songs, his most famous collaboration with her was his composition Inta Omri

Umm Kulthum broadcasting concerts via the radio 1934

After inaugurating Radio Cairo, Umm Kulthum holds monthly radio concerts. Her renown exploded as households all across the Middle East and North Africa considered these broadcasts as almost sacred.z

world of umm kulthum

Umm Kulthum Sang in Many Countries, These Are Some of Them

Egypt

Egypt | 1898-1975 Umm Kulthum performaces across the Arab world and beyond spanned over fifty years, from when she sang with her father at special occasions in the Nile Delta until her final illness in Cairo.

Tunisia

Tunisia | May 30-June9,1968 On the one-year anniversary of the Six-Day war, Umm Kulthum shared a prayer ritual with first lady of Tunisia. The emotional photos were shown throughout the Arab world, demonstrating solidarity between Tunisia and Egypt.

Morocco

Morocco | March 1- 21, 1968 To highlight the common faith uniting Arabs,she celebrated Eid Al Adha, an important Islamic holiday, with a needy Moroccan family. Moroccan citizens her show of shared faith by presenting her with a green candle representing Moses staff (walking stick).

Libya

Libya | March 10-22, 1969 In 1969, Muammar al-Gaddafi and his friends were ready to stage a coup of the Libyan government and set the date for March 12th. However, it turned out that Umm Kulthum was scheduled to perform that night. Out of respect for her and her songs dedicated to the Arab cause, Gaddafi postponed the revolution until she left Libya.

Palestine

Palestine 1945 Umm Kulthum appeared in concert in Palestine in 1945. She later performed two benefit concerts in support of Fatah, to help the political group attain a leadership role in the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO). The PLO was established to represent the Palestinian people.

France (Paris)

France (Paris) | November 9-22, 1967 Umm Kulthum performs for the first time outside the Arab World in Paris in 1967
